Credit & Loan Tips

Practical, actionable advice to improve your credit score and boost your loan approval chances.

1

Pay Bills on Time, Every Time

Payment history makes up roughly 35% of your credit score. Even one missed payment can drop your score significantly. Set up autopay or calendar reminders.

Impact on Score
Very High
2

Keep Credit Utilization Below 30%

If your credit limit is $10,000, try to keep your balance below $3,000. Lower utilization signals responsible borrowing. Below 10% is ideal.

Impact on Score
High
3

Don't Close Old Credit Cards

Length of credit history matters. Closing your oldest card shortens your average account age and reduces available credit, both hurting your score.

Impact on Score
Medium
4

Limit Hard Inquiries

Each loan or credit card application triggers a hard inquiry. Too many in a short period signals financial stress. Space out applications by at least 6 months.

Impact on Score
Medium
5

Diversify Your Credit Mix

Having different types of credit (credit card, auto loan, mortgage) shows lenders you can manage various obligations. Don't take on debt just for this though.

Impact on Score
Moderate
6

Check Your Credit Report for Errors

About 1 in 5 credit reports contain errors. Dispute inaccuracies with the credit bureau — a corrected error can boost your score instantly.

Impact on Score
High
7

Keep Your Debt-to-Income Ratio Low

Lenders look at how much of your income goes to debt payments. Aim for a DTI below 36%. Pay down existing debt before applying for new loans.

Impact on Approval
Very High
8

Build an Emergency Fund First

Having 3–6 months of expenses saved shows financial stability. Some lenders consider savings when making decisions, and it protects you from defaulting.

Impact on Approval
Medium
