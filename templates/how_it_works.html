How LoanSafe AI Works

We believe AI should be transparent. Here's exactly how our system makes predictions and why you can trust the results.

📝

1. You Enter Your Data

Age, income, credit score, desired loan amount, term, and employment status. We don't store your data — it's processed in real-time and never saved.

🤖

2. ML Model Predicts

A Logistic Regression model trained on loan approval data predicts whether your application would be approved or rejected. The model outputs probabilities for both outcomes.

📊

3. Confidence Scoring

Instead of just "yes" or "no," we calculate how confident the model is. If the model says "approved" with 95% confidence vs. 55% confidence, those are very different situations — and you should know the difference.

🔍

4. Data Drift Detection

We check if the data the model sees in production has shifted compared to its training data. If feature distributions have changed significantly, the model may not be reliable — and we tell you.

🛡️

5. Trust Decision

Combining confidence and drift status, the system assigns one of three trust levels:

AUTO APPROVE Confidence ≥ 80% and no drift → safe to trust
REVIEW REQUIRED Confidence 60-80% or drift detected → seek expert review
DO NOT TRUST Confidence < 60% → prediction unreliable
💡

6. Personalized Tips

Based on your specific inputs, you get tailored advice — like improving your credit score, adjusting loan amounts, or considering a different term — to improve your approval chances.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a real loan approval?

No. LoanSafe AI is an educational tool that estimates your eligibility based on a machine learning model. It is not affiliated with any bank or lending institution. Always consult a financial professional for real lending decisions.

Do you store my data?

No. Your inputs are processed in real-time to generate predictions and tips. Nothing is saved to disk or any database. Uploaded CSV files are analyzed in memory and not stored.

What model do you use?

We use Logistic Regression from scikit-learn, a well-established algorithm for binary classification. It's chosen for its interpretability and reliability.

What is data drift?

Data drift occurs when the statistical properties of the data the model receives in production differ from the training data. This can cause predictions to become unreliable. Our system detects this and warns you.

Can I use my own dataset?

Yes! Go to the Analyze Data page and upload a CSV with the required columns. The system will train a model on your data and generate a full trustworthiness report.
